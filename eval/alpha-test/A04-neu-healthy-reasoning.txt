After failing my first attempt at the AWS certification, I took a step back to analyze what went wrong. I realized I was strong on the conceptual questions but weak on the scenario-based ones that required applying multiple services together. So I changed my approach: instead of re-reading the study guide, I set up a personal AWS account and built three small projects that combined the services I was weakest on. I also started explaining concepts out loud to test whether I truly understood them or just recognized them. My second attempt went much better — I passed, though I still have areas to improve, particularly around cost optimization strategies.
