MIRA AdultEdge

Calibrated, theory-grounded diagnosis of judgment distortions in adult learning

Research Preview · v0.x

What MIRA Does

Adults learning hard skills routinely misdiagnose why they struggle — "I'm just bad at math," "I lack willpower," "it's this or nothing." These are not motivational slips; they are structured judgment distortions that quietly block improvement.

Worse, the usual remedy — asking peers online — often amplifies the distortion. In an informal internal analysis of public learning-advice threads (106 replies across 15 Reddit threads; single-annotator labeling — rigorous measurement is future work), peer responses reinforced the original bias about a third of the time and corrected it only about a fifth — consistent with external behavioral-science literature. MIRA exists to provide the calibrated, theory-grounded diagnosis that human advice networks usually don't.

What makes it different

1 · Original theory (PSR)

Built on an independent theory of hypothetical judgment that decomposes reasoning into Premise → Strategy → Result, locating where a distortion enters.

2 · Calibrated, not credulous

A neurosymbolic architecture: LLM candidates are verified by Prolog formal rules and rule-based critics. Designed to say "unverified" rather than over-claim. Calibration is the product.

3 · Original-theory-grounded taxonomy

A 21-pattern judgment-bias registry built on the author's PSR theory as its primary base, reinforced by cognitive-science anchors (ACT-R, Kahneman dual-process) and educational psychology, with pattern-specific Socratic coaching.

How to read: A learner's claim is first decomposed by PSR — pinpointing where in the reasoning (Premise / Strategy / Result) a distortion sits. The same claim is then checked by up to three independent lanes (rule-based / formal-logic / LLM), and verified or evidence-backed signals are surfaced — unverified candidates are explicitly marked as tentative. Detection and verification are deliberately separated, so MIRA can distinguish what it knows from what it merely suspects.

Safety gate: the report passes a violation check before output. Verdict language is blocked everywhere; willpower-blame language is blocked in all non-coaching sections. If Lane 2/3 are unavailable, a valid report is still produced from Lane 1 alone (graceful degradation).

How to read: MIRA is a dual-system design. System A diagnoses the learner — estimating learning stage, metacognitive quality, and task clarity across three axes — and generates Socratic coaching. System B detects reasoning patterns — matching against the 21-pattern registry and verifying them across lanes. The Shared Layer (PSR decomposition, typed contracts, safety-checked report) connects the two. Separating learner-diagnosis from pattern-detection lets each be verified on its own terms.

How to read: The 21 patterns are grouped by the kind of reasoning they distort — Deductive (a logical structure is misapplied), Inductive (evidence is overgeneralized), Abductive (causal attribution is distorted). Red edges mark patterns that frequently co-occur (compound); gray dashed edges mark look-alike pairs that need differential diagnosis. willpower_blame is special: MIRA detects it in a learner's input but is forbidden from ever producing it (Invariant #12) — encoding the principle that structure, not character, explains most failure.
